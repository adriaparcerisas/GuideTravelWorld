ZAMBIA - THE BIG FIVE
27 April | Mountain

Hi travelers!

On our first article we will talk about one of our recurrent dreams. The Big Five: buffalos, lions, leopards, rhinoceros and elephants.

Zambia is one of these africans countries where you can see the five magnific animals during your visits to the three most important national parks of the country: South Luangwa National Park, Lower Zambezi National Park and Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park. This animals are in danger of extinction due the human actions of the last century. On our eagerness to be the clever and powerful animal we develop the selfishness to show other humans how superiors we are, hunting this animals as a hobby and breaking the balance of the ecosystem.

Elephants have a very good memory, they can remember his mother after years and years without see her, and they are so important because they disperse the seed of plants, helps insects providing food, helps another animals to find water making holes on the floor and helps habitats to grow, eating plants from trees allowing sun rays arrive to the grass. Buffalos has an important role on predation, helps many birds on their reproduction cycle and regulates the grass growing eating it. On third place, rhinoceros are a good soil fertilizers with their abundant fecal and, as buffalos, are a grass eaters, helping to the grass growing regulation. Leopards are important hunters on the ecosystem, they eat everything as fish, reptiles, birds or mammals, regulating the growing population of them. And the last of the Big Five is our favourite, the lion, the jungle king; as leopards, are important hunters helping to the population growing control.

Of course, this five awesome animals are only a small part of all the African ecosystem, but if you can make a safari don’t miss the opportunity to discover this amazing wildlife. And remember, humans are only part of that world, we don’t have the power to control it, it does by itself!

Andrea & Cris
